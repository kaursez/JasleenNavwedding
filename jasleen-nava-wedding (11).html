Dress Code
Think lush, think layered, think the kind of outfit you'd wear to a wedding in the hills — because that's exactly where you're headed.

Here's a little direction for each moment:
Into the Wild — Mehendi
We're in the hills, surrounded by green — wear something that sets it on fire. Festive chaos, the kind that photographs beautifully.
Cocktail Evening
Think dark. Think royal. Think exuding elegance. This is your moment to dress like the hills dressed up for you.
Anand Karaj
Against a beautiful lakeside view, under an afternoon sun that reflects light off everything it touches — what would you wear? Think that. Bright, luminous, effortlessly radiant.
Muhurtham
Sarees for the women. For the men — dress Indian, feel a part of the culture, and honour the moment. This one calls for tradition worn with pride.
Travel & Getting Here
Naukuchiatal is approximately 6 hours from Delhi, 2 hours from Bareilly, and 1 hour from Pantnagar — the easiest route in is through Delhi.

We recommend driving up; the roads through the Kumaon hills make for a beautiful journey.

The nearest railway station is Kathgodam (roughly 30–40 mins from the venue), well-connected to both Delhi and Bareilly by train.

We'll share a detailed travel guide closer to October — reach out anytime if you need help planning your journey.

REEL TURNED INTO
Real Life.
Reel turned into Real life.
Jasleen has and is obsessed with 2 States as a movie and would cry EVERY SINGLE TIME hearing the song "Ullam Padam".
Little did she know she manifested that in the most beautiful way — when she moved to Bangalore for work, she never thought she'd fall for a guy like Nav, quite opposite to what she knew of.
Nav, who only knew how to work and didn't spare time for anything else, slowly found himself caring for her.
And then — quietly, naturally —. What started as friendship grew into something neither of them saw coming, until they realised they weren't just colleagues at StockGro anymore. They were home to each other.
When Nava proposed in 2023, Jasleen had a moment of sudden clarity: she had manifested this. Back in 2014, after watching 2 States for the first time, a Punjabi girl had quietly hoped for exactly this kind of love — the cross-cultural, utterly cinematic, slightly unbelievable kind. A Sikh and a Telugu, meeting over work in Bangalore, falling in love over chai and filter coffee.

"Ik cup cha met filter coffee…
and they fell in love!"

Cheers to the couple who found comfort away from home in each other and are starting this beautiful journey together!
Jasleen always wanted those heartfelt, larger-than-life moments. And well, Nava? He wants what makes her happy.
And now, in the hills of Uttarakhand, with their families, their cultures, and their people around them, they're ready to begin the next chapter.
